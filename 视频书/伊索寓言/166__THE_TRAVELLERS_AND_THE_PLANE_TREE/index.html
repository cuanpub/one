The And The Plane-Tree

Two were walking along a and road a summer's day. Coming to a Plane-tree, they turnedaside to of the sun shade of its spreading branches. Asthey rested, looking up into the tree, one ofthem remarked to his , "What a tree the Plane is! It bears no fruit and is ofno service to man at all. The Plane-tree him with . You creature! it cried: "you come and under me from the sun, and then, in thevery act of enjoying shade of my, you abuse me and call me good fornothing!

Many a service is met with.
